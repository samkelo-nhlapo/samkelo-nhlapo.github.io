🏥 HealthcareForm

A full‑stack healthcare management system with SQL Server, ASP.NET Core (.NET 10) API, and Angular 20 frontend.

Overview

HealthcareForm is a comprehensive platform designed for healthcare providers to manage patient data, appointments, medical records, billing, and compliance. It features role‑based access control, JWT authentication, and a responsive Angular UI.

Key Features

  • Patient Hub – Central record with demographics, medical history, allergies, medications, vaccinations, and lab results.
  • Forms & Submissions – Dynamic form templates, submissions, field values, and attachments (intake, consent, etc.).
  • Healthcare Services – Appointments, consultation notes (SOAP), referrals, and provider management.
  • Insurance & Billing – Patient insurance, invoices, billing codes (ICD‑10, CPT), and payment tracking.
  • Security & Access Control – User roles, permissions, and a comprehensive audit log (UserActivityAudit).

Database Design (34 Tables, 3NF)

The database is normalized to 3NF and includes:

  • Medical Records: MedicalHistory, Allergies, Medications, Vaccinations, LabResults
  • Forms: FormTemplates, FormSubmissions, FormFieldValues, FormAttachments
  • Services: HealthcareProviders, Appointments, ConsultationNotes, Referrals
  • Insurance: InsuranceProviders, PatientInsurance, Invoices, BillingCodes
  • Security: Users, Roles, Permissions, UserRoles, RolePermissions, UserActivityAudit
  • Lookups: Gender, MaritalStatus, Countries, Provinces, Cities, Address, Phones, Emails

Total indexes: 45+ – optimized for search performance on ID_Number, LastName, Status, and foreign keys. The schema supports complex reporting and analytics.

Data Analysis Capabilities

  • Patient visit frequency and no‑show rates.
  • Prescribing patterns and medication adherence.
  • Revenue and outstanding invoice analysis.
  • Audit trail for compliance (HIPAA‑style).
  • Lab result trends over time.

Sample Query

-- Count of patients with abnormal lab results by month
SELECT 
    YEAR(lr.CompletedDate) AS Year,
    MONTH(lr.CompletedDate) AS Month,
    COUNT(*) AS AbnormalResults
FROM Profile.LabResults lr
WHERE lr.Status = 'Abnormal'
GROUP BY YEAR(lr.CompletedDate), MONTH(lr.CompletedDate)
ORDER BY Year DESC, Month DESC;
